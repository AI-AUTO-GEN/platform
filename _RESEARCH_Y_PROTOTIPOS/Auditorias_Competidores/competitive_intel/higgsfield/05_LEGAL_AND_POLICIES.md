# Higgsfield AI — Legal & Policies Summary
> Audit Date: 2026-04-24 | Sources: higgsfield.ai/terms-of-use-agreement, higgsfield.ai/privacy-policy

## Content Ownership

### User Retains Ownership
- Higgsfield **does NOT claim ownership** of User Inputs (prompts, uploaded images) or Outputs (generated content)
- Users retain full ownership of generated content

### Commercial Use
- Higgsfield **does NOT restrict** commercial use of outputs
- Users can use generated content commercially without additional licensing

### License Grant to Higgsfield
- Users grant Higgsfield a **"perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free"** license to:
  - Use, modify, and display content
  - For service operation and marketing purposes
- This is a broad license — content you generate can be used by Higgsfield for promotional purposes

## AI Training Data Usage

### ⚠️ Critical: User Data for Training
- Terms (Section 2.3) and Privacy Policy explicitly state:
  - Higgsfield uses **user-shared text** for training
  - Higgsfield uses **multimedia data** (photos/videos) for training
  - Higgsfield uses **prompt data** for training
- This means: Everything you input and generate may be used to train their models

## Privacy Policy Highlights

### Data Collected
- Account information (email, name)
- Usage data (prompts, generation history, preferences)
- Device information (browser, OS, IP)
- Payment information (processed via third-party)
- Uploaded media (photos, videos for Soul ID training)

### Data Sharing
- With service providers (hosting, payment, analytics)
- For legal compliance
- In connection with business transfers
- Aggregated/anonymized data may be shared publicly

### Data Retention
- Account data retained while account is active
- Generated content retained per platform policies
- Training data: unclear retention/deletion policy

## Implications for Our Platform (Renderfarm)

### Competitive Opportunities
1. **Opt-out Training:** We could differentiate by NOT training on user data (privacy-first)
2. **Stronger IP Protection:** Offer more limited platform license grant
3. **Data Sovereignty:** Local processing / self-hosted options
4. **Transparent Data Practices:** Clear, plain-language data policies

### Risks to Understand
1. The "perpetual, irrevocable" license is industry-standard but aggressive
2. Training on user prompts is common but increasingly controversial
3. Commercial use freedom is expected by professional users
4. No clear content deletion guarantee for training data
